Alumni Success Stories

Hilda Zet

~Class of 2010, Bachelor of Science in Computer Science

Hilda Zet began her career at a top tech company shortly after graduating with honors and worked her way up the ranks. Leaders in the field were drawn to her skills in AI and machine learning and she eventually started her own company with the goal of creating innovative AI solutions for the medical field. Since then, Hilda's business has been acknowledged for its creative work in early disease diagnosis and she has grown to be well-known in the tech and medical fields. Hilda is committed to promoting diversity in the tech industry and frequently visits the department to assist young computer scientists.


Aiden Anter

~Class of 2016, Bachelor of Science in Information Technology

Acknowledged for his extraordinary abilities, Aiden was hired by a multinational cybersecurity company, where he proceeded to create noteworthy advancements in the industry. He advanced through the ranks over time and eventually held the position of Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) for a large organization. In this capacity, Aiden is in charge of managing the business's cybersecurity plan and guaranteeing the privacy of critical data.

Alumni Spotlight of the Year

Meet Donetta Dodoh, a notable alumnus of our department of computer science who has advanced the area of cybersecurity to great heights. Since earning her honors degree in computer science in 2010, Sarah has blazed a new path in the field of cybersecurity. Donetta joined a top cybersecurity company after earning her degree, and her enthusiasm for protecting digital environments helped her advance into leadership positions there. Experts in the field were impressed by her creative approach to countering new cyberthreats, and she was recognized for her achievements.


Among Donetta's noteworthy accomplishments was her part in creating a state-of-the-art intrusion detection system, which is now a mainstay of contemporary cybersecurity procedures. Her efforts improved an organization's security posture and established a new benchmark for proactive threat identification. Apart from her career achievements, Donetta is a strong supporter of diversity in the cybersecurity field. She has taken an active position in outreach initiatives aimed at inspiring women and other underrepresented groups to seek employment in the industry. The cybersecurity community has seen positive change as a result of Donetta's dedication to diversity.


By giving guest lectures on cybersecurity trends, imparting her knowledge to current students, and actively engaging in alumni activities, Donetta maintains her connection to our department. Her transformation from a student in our computer science program to a prominent cybersecurity expert inspires future cybersecurity experts in our community. We are honored to have Donetta Dodoh as one of our own, and her continued contributions to the cybersecurity community are a shining example of the quality and creativity we work hard to instill in each and every one of our graduates.

Donetta Dodoh
