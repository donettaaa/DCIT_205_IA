Insight Into Undergraduate Courses

The Department of Computer Science offers two undergraduate degree programs: The Bachelor of Science in Information Technology and the Bachelor of Science in Computer Science Single-Major and Major-Minor. In order to be eligible for admission to Level 200 Computer Science programs, applicants must have completed the following Level 100 courses:

MATH 121: Algebra and Trigonometry

MATH 122: Calculus I CSCD 101: Introduction to Computer Science I

CSCD 102: Introduction to Computer Science II

Every student in the Department of Computer Science must own his or her laptop with minimum specification of:

i3 64-bit Processor

8GB RAM

500GB HD or 256 SSD

Insight Into MPhil and MSc Programmes

Currently, the University of Ghana's Department of Computer Science offers Master of Philosophy (MPhil) and Master of Science (MSc) programs in computer science.

While the MPhil is a two-year, full-time program, the MSc is a one-year, weekend/evening program with a capstone project. The MPhil program consists of two years: a teaching first year and a research and dissertation year.

These two expedited master's programs are intended to train future and present computer scientists who can pursue full- or part-time education.

Students enrolled in these programs have advanced technical skills and a better understanding of theoretical computer science, enabling them to view technology as a disruptive force in managing complex worldwide societal issues.

Course: Introduction to Programming

Learning how to program is the first step towards a career in computer science. The fundamentals of coding are taught in this course, which also introduces students to the syntax and organization used to control computers.Students write lines of code to tackle real-world problems through practical projects. The training breaks down programming language into easily understood building components, much like building with Legos. Giving pupils the tools they need for complex programming and practical applications is the aim.In addition to imparting technical knowledge, the training cultivates an analytical and problem-solving approach. It's the secret to opening up the digital world and enabling everybody to contribute to the limitless potential of programming. It's similar like mastering the alphabet before exploring the vast realm of narrative.

Course: Calculus I & II

Calculus is based on the idea of a limit, which enables us to comprehend and measure how functions behave as they approach particular values. The analysis of a function's variation at any given point is made possible by derivatives, which indicate rates of change. Conversely, integrals make it easier to compute accumulated amounts or areas under curves. Numerous fields, including physics, computer science, engineering, economics, and biology, use calculus extensively. Calculus offers an effective foundation for resolving issues in the real world, from projectile trajectory modeling to complex system optimization. Because calculus can quantify change and characterize dynamic processes, it is an essential instrument for scientific research and technological development.

Course: Software Engineering

The dynamic and ever-evolving profession of software engineering is vital to forming our world's digital environment. It includes the methodical design, development, testing, and upkeep of software systems, with the ultimate objective being the provision of effective and high-quality solutions to satisfy a wide range of needs. Software engineering is fundamentally a methodical approach to problem-solving. It entails comprehending customer requirements, deciphering intricate issues, and creating reliable, expandable solutions. Careful planning and requirement analysis are frequently the first steps in this process, which creates a clear development roadmap.
